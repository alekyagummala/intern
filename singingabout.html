SINGING FOR LIFE

  • Classical Singing:

    Characterized by a formal and structured approach, classical singing focuses on techniques such as breath control, vocal agility, and precise pitch. It includes opera, art songs, and oratorio performances.

  • Rock Singing:

    Rock singing involves powerful and often raspy vocals, with an emphasis on energy and intensity. It includes a wide range of sub-genres, from classic rock to heavy metal, each with its vocal characteristics.
  • Trained under the guidance of renowned ballet masters and mentors

  • Pop Singing:

    Pop singing is diverse and encompasses various styles within popular music. It often features a more relaxed vocal style, with an emphasis on expression, emotion, and storytelling.

  • Jazz Singing:

    Jazz singing is characterized by improvisation, scat singing, and a strong connection to rhythm. Vocalists in jazz explore unique phrasing and use their voices as instruments in ensemble settings.
